The Three Methods

Method A

Full Market Value

Treats stocks as trading goods. Zakat is due on the full market value of the portfolio, regardless of the company's underlying assets.

Value × 2.5%
Method B

Zakatable Assets (30% proxy)

Treats stocks as partial ownership of a company. Only the zakatable assets (cash, receivables, inventory) are dutiable — approximated at 30% of market value.

Value × 30% × 2.5%
Method C

Dividends Only

For long-term passive investors who do not intend to sell. Some scholars hold that only received dividends are zakatable — not the full market value of shares.

Dividends × 2.5%

The 30% is a scholarly proxy when company balance sheet data is unavailable. (IFG, Qaradawi)

Why the difference matters: Method A always yields significantly more than Method B. Most online calculators only show you one method. Zakat Al-Mall shows all three so you can make an informed decision — or consult a scholar.
