The Two Methods

Method A

Market Value

AAOIFI SS 35 · NZF · Islamic Relief

Treats stocks as trading goods. Zakat is calculated on the full market value of the portfolio, regardless of what the company owns.

Method B

Underlying Assets

IFG Guide · Qaradawi · Hanafi/Shafi'i scholars

Treats stocks as ownership stakes. Zakat is calculated only on the company's zakatable assets (cash, inventory, receivables), not the full market value.

The 30% is a scholarly proxy for zakatable assets when company balance sheet data is unavailable. (Source: IFG, Qaradawi)

The key insight: Same shares. Same price. Same day. Two valid scholarly methods. Method A always yields 233% more than Method B. Most online calculators only show you one.
