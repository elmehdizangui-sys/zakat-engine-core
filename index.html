The Two Methods

Same shares. Same price. Same day. Two valid scholarly positions.

Method A — Full Market Value

Treat Stocks as Trading Goods

Treats stocks as trading goods. Zakat is due on the full market value of the portfolio, regardless of the company's underlying assets.

Portfolio Value × 2.5%

· Qaradawi · Hanafi/Shafi'i scholars

Method B — Underlying Assets

Treat Stocks as Ownership Stakes

Treats stocks as partial ownership of a company. Only the zakatable assets of the company (cash, receivables, inventory) are dutiable — approximated at 30% of market value.

Portfolio Value × 30% × 2.5%

· AAOIFI Standard No. 35 · IFG

The 30% is a scholarly proxy when company balance sheet data is unavailable. (IFG, Qaradawi)

Why the difference matters: Method A always yields significantly more than Method B. Most online calculators only show you one method. ZakatEngine shows both so you can make an informed decision — or consult a scholar.
